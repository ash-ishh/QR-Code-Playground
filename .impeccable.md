## Design Context

### Users
Broad audience: anyone who needs to generate a QR code quickly. In practice this includes creators, small businesses, marketers, event organizers, restaurants, and operators who need a link or message turned into a branded, scannable QR without design friction.

Primary jobs:
- generate a QR from a URL or text fast
- make it look clean and intentional
- keep it reliably scannable
- export it for web, print, or social
- optionally prepare it for video placement

### Brand Personality
Simple, minimal, adaptive.

The interface should feel calm, efficient, and confident rather than flashy. It should reduce decision fatigue and make QR creation feel straightforward.

### Aesthetic Direction
A restrained editorial-product aesthetic with strong whitespace, precise typography, low visual noise, and adaptive theming. Avoid generic dark SaaS dashboards, glassmorphism, neon gradients, and over-carded layouts.

### Design Principles
1. Simplicity first: remove visual noise before adding emphasis.
2. Make the QR preview the focal point.
3. Use hierarchy through spacing and typography, not decoration.
4. Keep controls practical and readable for fast task completion.
5. Support both light and dark contexts without changing the product’s calm tone.
